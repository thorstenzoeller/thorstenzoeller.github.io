My Text Playlist

2022-01-08

This is my text playlist, a well-curated list of articles which are highly relevant to me and which I re-read back on a more or less regular basis.

I stumbled across the idea of a text playlist when Leo Babauta shared his; he in turn credits Liz Danzico, who references Frank Chimero (the link to his original text playlist seems to be broken).

I like the concept of a text playlist, since it forces you to think about which of the many great articles and writings out there are not just interesting, but truly essential to you – those articles you seem to come back to over and over again. The idea with a text playlist is to pare down all the great articles you come across to just those few really essential ones. I encourage you to spend some time and create your own.

So here is my current text playlist:

  • Learning to Sit Alone, in a Quiet Empty Room by Leo Babauta.

  • Subtract by Derek Sivers.

  • Consistency Beats Talent, Luck, Good Intentions, and Even Quality by Anthony Moore.

  • Nothing is Ever Hidden by Brad Warner.

  • How I Got My Attention Back by Craig Mod.

  • Ashtanga Yoga is not fun by Shareen Woodford.
